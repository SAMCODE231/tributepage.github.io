Joseph Jenkins Roberts Life Timeline:

  • March 15, 1809: Jseph Jenkins Roberts was born in Norfolk, Virginia, United States.
  • 1829: Roberts emigrated to Liberia, a colony established by the American Colonization Society as a destination for free African Americans and former slaves.
  • 1833:He became a successful merchant and landholder in Liberia, gaining prominence within the growing community.
  • 1841: Roberts was appointed as the first black governor of the colony of Liberia.
  • January 3, 1848:
  • Liberia declared its independence from the American Colonization Society, and Joseph Jenkins Roberts was elected as the first President of Liberia.
  • January 7, 1848: Roberts was inaugurated as the first President of Liberia, leading the young nation during its formative years.
  • 1852: He was re-elected for a second term as President.
  • 1856:strong> Roberts was re-elected once again, serving a third term as President of Liberia.
  • 1862: He declined to run for re-election, ending his third term in office.

  • 1883: Joseph Jenkins Roberts resigned from the presidency for health reasons and retired from public life.
  • February 24, 1876: Joseph Jenkins Roberts passed away in Liberia.

Founding Father: Roberts is often referred to as one of the founding fathers of Liberia. He played a key role in the country's establishment as an independent nation and its early development. His leadership during the critical formative years of Liberia is widely acknowledged.
